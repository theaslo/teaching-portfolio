Research, Scholarship, and Creative Work

My research focuses on theoretical nuclear physics, particularly the structure of hadrons through analytic and model-based parton distribution functions. I work with the Covariant Parton Model and analytic solutions to the DGLAP evolution equations to understand the momentum and spin structure of mesons.

Current Research Directions

  • Mentoring Thomas Tarutin and Devesh Kartnik on pion and kaon distributions in the Covariant Parton Model
  • Developing nondiagonal DVCS and DVMP observables for future EIC physics programs
  • Preparing publication on kaon PDFs based on analytic modeling

Student Research Involvement

I have mentored undergraduate students on competitive projects that resulted in awards, conference presentations, and graduate admissions. Former student Matthew Markovich worked on DGLAP evolution and is now pursuing a Ph.D. at Stony Brook.

Pedagogical Innovation

I develop Model Context Protocol (MCP) tools that support GenAI integration in physics learning. These tools enable guided problem-solving, conceptual development, and computational support across instruction and research mentorship.

Goals for the Next 5–10 Years

  • Expand analytic modeling of parton distributions, including kaon and pion structure
  • Contribute to phenomenological predictions for exclusive EIC processes
  • Advance the use of MCP tools in undergraduate physics education and coding literacy
  • Grow collaborations around AI-assisted research workflows and computation
